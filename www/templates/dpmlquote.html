Scope creep is real.

I found that the deeper I got into mapping out and automating the certain license relationships the more effort it took just to make the application function. Unfortuantely, the Machine Learning portion, while still being developed, is not quite "ready for primetime" so-to-speak, and so the release date is being pushed back a bit. These license relationships didn't seem to exist anywhere besides in some seemingly well hidden written documentation and some people's heads. The screen shot of that table below is multiple days worth of research and confirmation, and is the beginnings of a usable pre/co-requirements framework that will enable much more intelligent quoting services, that may be able to even make particular reccommendations based on user input. Or begin constructing a pre-filled Statement of Work for client approval.

This tool will save time, significantly reduce the occurance of errors and omissions on sales orders, highlight areas where software pricing models could stand to be improved, and provide solid analyitics and deeper sales pipeline tracking through potential integrations with KMBS's implementation of SAP CRM360.
